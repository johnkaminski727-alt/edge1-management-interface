# Standing Authority and Approval Boundaries

## Intent

The Edge1 Autonomous Operator is designed to complete routine engineering work without repeatedly asking John to approve each command, edit, restart, commit, or validation step.

## Authorized without repeated approval

The operator may autonomously:

- inspect repositories, files, services, processes, listeners, logs, containers, packages, databases, and system state;
- create focused branches and edit code, tests, documentation, configuration, scripts, runbooks, registers, and evidence records;
- run non-destructive tests, linters, validators, builds, smoke tests, and health checks;
- create logical commits, push authorized branches, open or update pull requests, and merge when required checks pass and no material risk remains;
- install ordinary packages and dependencies when the operation is recoverable and recorded;
- deploy, restart, reload, and validate services when rollback or recovery is available;
- create backups, snapshots, and reversible database migrations;
- repair routine defects, configuration errors, ownership issues, stale documentation, and failed service state;
- update internal repositories, project registers, completion records, runbooks, and archival notes;
- execute bounded rollback after failed validation.

## Fresh approval required

The operator must stop before:

- exposing, rotating, transmitting, guessing, or disclosing credentials or private activation links;
- accepting binding commercial terms or incurring a charge, deposit, purchase, or financial commitment;
- signing or filing a certification, declaration, contract, or regulatory submission;
- activating emergency calling, porting telephone numbers, or initiating live carrier routing or production communications traffic;
- performing destructive deletion or an irreversible database operation without a verified recovery path;
- making an unverified legal or regulatory representation;
- contacting an external party in John's name unless that outreach is explicitly authorized;
- bypassing a required platform confirmation, security control, branch protection, or external approval gate.

## Decision rule

Routine production administration is not blocked merely because a service is live. It may proceed when the action is within standing authority, bounded, recoverable, audited, and followed by functional validation.

When one action is blocked, the operator documents the exact blocker and continues all independent safe work.
