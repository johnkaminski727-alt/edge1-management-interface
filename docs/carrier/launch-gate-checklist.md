# WW.CX Carrier Launch Gate Checklist

A checked box must point to retained evidence. This checklist does not replace CRTC confirmation or legal advice.

## Gate 1 — Corporate and regulatory eligibility

- [ ] Canadian applicant entity is confirmed.
- [ ] Ownership and control assessment is complete.
- [ ] Initial service catalogue and customer classes are approved.
- [ ] Initial provinces, exchanges, and rate centres are approved.
- [ ] CLEC Type IV/III/I classification is confirmed.
- [ ] Response Manager and alternate are appointed.
- [ ] Telecommunications provider registration is complete.
- [ ] BITS applicability is resolved and licence obtained if required.

## Gate 2 — Proposed CLEC recognition

- [ ] Proposed CLEC filing and exhibits are submitted.
- [ ] CRTC questions are answered.
- [ ] Proposed CLEC recognition is received.
- [ ] Underlying Canadian LEC is selected.
- [ ] Delegated-obligation matrix is contractually accepted.
- [ ] Applicable model tariff is prepared and filed.
- [ ] Required agreements are filed or approved.

## Gate 3 — Technical and operational acceptance

- [ ] Number inventory is reconciled and controlled.
- [ ] Successful test ports are completed in both directions.
- [ ] 9-1-1/NG9-1-1 agreement is active.
- [ ] Customer location synchronization is tested.
- [ ] Controlled emergency calling tests pass.
- [ ] STIR/SHAKEN signing and verification tests pass.
- [ ] SIP interconnect acceptance suite passes.
- [ ] TLS renewal and replacement are tested.
- [ ] Fraud controls and destination limits are active.
- [ ] Essential services have a tested failure-isolated path.
- [ ] Backup restoration and disaster recovery are tested.
- [ ] Synthetic calling and infrastructure monitoring are active.
- [ ] NOC escalation and major-outage tabletop exercises pass.
- [ ] Security and privacy assessments are approved.
- [ ] Billing and partner CDR reconciliation tests pass.

## Gate 4 — Commercial launch authorization

- [ ] CRTC confirms completion of applicable CLEC requirements.
- [ ] CCTS participation is active.
- [ ] Customer agreement and critical disclosures are approved.
- [ ] Emergency-service acknowledgement is retained during signup.
- [ ] Port-out and cancellation processes are operational.
- [ ] Accessibility obligations and feedback channel are operational.
- [ ] Support and on-call staff are trained.
- [ ] No critical authorization, emergency-service, security, or resilience risk remains open.
- [ ] Internal pilot meets approved quality thresholds.
- [ ] Invitation pilot meets approved quality thresholds.
- [ ] Leadership signs the limited commercial launch record.

## Immediate stop conditions

Pause new activations and invoke incident governance for:

- any systemic 9-1-1 routing or location defect;
- loss of lawful operating authorization;
- uncontrolled number-routing or portability failure;
- unauthorized STIR/SHAKEN attestation;
- uncontrolled toll fraud;
- material billing corruption;
- inability to support port-out;
- a major security or privacy breach;
- recurring severe outage without effective containment.
